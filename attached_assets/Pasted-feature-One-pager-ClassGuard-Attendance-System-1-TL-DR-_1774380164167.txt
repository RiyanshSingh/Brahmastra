feature : One-pager: ClassGuard Attendance System

1. TL;DR

ClassGuard is a real-time attendance verification system that prevents proxy attendance in universities by combining location tracking, WiFi connectivity monitoring, and distance verification. Unlike traditional attendance systems where students can mark attendance and leave, ClassGuard enables teachers to verify student presence throughout the class session, ensuring only physically present students receive attendance credit.




2. Goals

Business Goals

Reduce proxy attendance incidents by 95% within the first semester of implementation

Increase actual classroom attendance rates by 30% through verified presence tracking

Provide universities with accurate attendance data for compliance and accreditation purposes

Create a scalable solution that can be deployed across multiple institutions

User Goals

Enable teachers to take and verify attendance quickly without disrupting class flow

Allow teachers to identify absent students in real-time during class sessions

Give students a simple, friction-free way to mark attendance when physically present

Provide administrators with reliable attendance reports and analytics

Non-Goals

Building a complete learning management system (LMS) with grades, assignments, or course materials

Implementing biometric authentication (fingerprint, facial recognition) in the initial version

Creating a social platform for student communication

Tracking student movement outside of class hours or designated learning spaces

3. User stories

Professor Sarah needs to ensure students attend her 200-person lecture hall class because attendance impacts course completion rates. She wants to verify that students who marked attendance are actually present throughout the session without manually calling names, which would waste 15 minutes of class time.




Student Alex attends class regularly and wants a quick way to mark attendance without complicated check-ins. He's frustrated that his attendance record looks the same as classmates who leave after marking present.




Department Administrator Michelle needs accurate attendance data for accreditation reports and to identify at-risk students early. She requires reliable data that can't be manipulated by proxy attendance schemes.




Student Jordan occasionally arrives 10 minutes late due to transportation issues and worries about being marked absent even though she attends the full class. She needs a system that accounts for reasonable arrival windows.




4. Functional requirements

Must Have (P0)

Student roster display showing name, roll number, and attendance status for each class session

Real-time location tracking showing student's current location relative to classroom

Distance calculation displaying each student's proximity to the teacher's device in meters

WiFi connectivity status showing whether student is connected to campus network

One-tap attendance marking interface for students

Teacher dashboard showing all three verification signals (location, distance, WiFi) per student

Mid-class verification allowing teachers to re-check attendance at any point during the session

Attendance override capability for teachers to manually mark present/absent with notes

Should Have (P1)

Geofencing that auto-marks students present when they enter the classroom radius

Attendance history view showing patterns over the semester

Push notifications to students when attendance window opens and closes

Bulk actions for teachers to mark multiple students absent/present

Export functionality for attendance reports in CSV format

Grace period settings allowing 10-15 minute arrival buffer

Could Have (P2)

Analytics dashboard showing attendance trends, at-risk students, and class averages

Integration with existing university student information systems

QR code backup for situations where location services fail

Offline mode that syncs data when connection returns

Multi-section support for teachers handling multiple class sections

Won't Have (Initial Release)

Grade tracking or assignment submission

Video recording or lecture capture

Payment or fee collection functionality

5. User experience

Teacher Journey

Opens ClassGuard app and selects today's class section from schedule

Views student roster with real-time status indicators (green = verified present, yellow = questionable, red = absent)

Taps "Start Attendance" to open the 15-minute check-in window

Students mark attendance on their devices within the window

Teacher monitors the live dashboard showing three verification signals per student

Mid-class (30 minutes in), teacher taps "Verify Attendance" to re-check all students

System flags students whose location/WiFi status has changed significantly

At class end, teacher reviews final attendance list and manually adjusts any edge cases

Submits final attendance with one tap

Student Journey

Arrives at classroom and connects to campus WiFi

Receives push notification that attendance is open

Opens ClassGuard app and taps class name

Taps "Mark Present" button (one action)

Sees confirmation that attendance is recorded

Stays in classroom as location is passively monitored

No further action needed unless teacher runs mid-class verification

Edge Cases & UI Notes

Late arrivals: System allows teachers to set grace period (default 15 minutes). Students arriving within grace period can still mark attendance.

WiFi issues: If campus WiFi is down, system falls back to cellular location tracking alone. Teacher sees warning indicator.

GPS inaccuracy: Distance threshold set to 50 meters to account for GPS drift. Teachers can see exact distance reading for manual judgment.

Students leaving mid-class: When student's location moves beyond geofence, status changes to yellow "Questionable." Teacher decides whether to mark absent.

Device without location services: Student must enable location to use app. Clear onboarding screens explain privacy policy and necessity.

Classroom basement/poor signal: Teacher can manually mark students present with photo documentation backup option.

6. Narrative

It's 9:00 AM on Tuesday, and Professor Sarah walks into her Engineering 101 lecture hall where 180 students are registered. She opens ClassGuard and taps "Start Attendance" for this section. Students' phones buzz with the notification.




Alex, already seated in the third row, pulls out his phone, opens the app, and taps "Mark Present" on the Engineering 101 tile. It takes three seconds. The app confirms he's connected to campus WiFi "CampusNet-Building4" and is 12 meters from Professor Sarah's device. His name turns green on her dashboard.




Meanwhile, Marcus is trying something different. He opens the app from his dorm room across campus, hoping to mark present and skip class. He taps "Mark Present" but the app shows his distance from the classroom as 2.3 kilometers. The system flags him as "Not in Range" and doesn't record attendance. His name stays red on Professor Sarah's screen.




By 9:15 AM, Professor Sarah sees 165 students marked present with verified signals. She notes 15 absent students and starts her lecture. At 9:45 AM, during a break between topics, she taps "Verify Attendance" to run a mid-class check. The system re-evaluates all students' locations and WiFi status.




The dashboard shows four yellow flags. Three students' locations have shifted beyond the geofence—they likely left after marking attendance. One student's WiFi disconnected but location shows still in the building (probably switched to cellular data). Professor Sarah makes a quick mental note and continues teaching, planning to review these cases after class.




Jessica rushes in at 9:20 AM, late because her bus broke down. She immediately marks her attendance. Professor Sarah's screen shows Jessica is within the grace period and properly located in the classroom. With one tap, Sarah approves the late arrival.




At the end of class, Professor Sarah reviews the final attendance: 162 verified present, 3 questionable (she marks them absent), 15 absent. She adds a note about Jessica's late arrival and submits. The entire process took less than two minutes of her attention across the 90-minute class.




The next morning, Department Head Michelle opens her admin dashboard and sees Engineering 101's actual attendance rate is 89%—lower than the 97% that was being reported under the old honor system. Now she has accurate data to implement early intervention programs for chronically absent students.
